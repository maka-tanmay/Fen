Not CRM CRM

A pipeline was built for deals. This was built for people who come back — or should have.

Traditional CRMs assume a relationship ends at a sale. Practice-management EHRs assume a relationship is just a series of appointments. Neither one notices the space in between, where trust is either kept warm or quietly lost.

Two nodes, one system

Every relationship in the practice reduces to one of two shapes.

Node one — the client

Drifts away slowly

Rarely a cancellation, never an announcement — the gap between sessions just quietly widens until they're gone. Fen watches cadence, not just the calendar, and flags the drift while there's still time to reach out.

Node two — the referring doctor

Builds through natural contact

Referral relationships don't grow on their own — they grow on being acknowledged. Fen tracks who sent what, when they last heard back, and nudges the loop closed before goodwill quietly runs dry.

The core belief

Relationship, before revenue. Every decision in this product answers to that first.

Revenue follows relationships that are actually maintained — not the other way around.
