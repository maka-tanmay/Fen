Internal reference — v1

Every choice here answers to a reason, not a preference.

This is the working brand kit for fen — the name, the palette, the type, and the voice, along with why each one was chosen and what it was deliberately built to avoid.

The name

fen — Foundation for Enduring Networks

Short, abstract, and built for the professional-tool category — closer to Attio, Folk, and Dex than to a wellness app.

  • Category fit. Comparable products are relationship-intelligence CRMs, not consumer wellness apps — the name needed to read as a serious business tool, not a self-care product.
  • Built-in double meaning. A fen is a wetland of reeds — the logo mark reads as reeds and as a sparkline at once, which is literally what the cadence-tracking feature produces.
  • Checked, not assumed. Aro, Faro, and Kip were all considered and ruled out after a direct-conflict check — one of them (Kipu) is an existing CRM built for behavioral health, the exact adjacent space fen occupies.
Note: the conflict check above was a lightweight web search, not formal trademark or domain clearance. That step still needs to happen before anything goes public.

Color

Mist, moss, and rust — not cream and clay.

Light and calm was always the goal. But the first pass landed on warm cream plus a terracotta accent, which is close to the most common AI-generated design fingerprint right now — and close to Claude's own interaction color. The palette was rebuilt around a cooler, greener base to read as its own thing.

Type

A warm serif for headings, a plain sans for everything dense.

Fraunces carries the brand's personality without sliding into softness. Inter stays out of the way on data-heavy screens — rosters, referral maps, anything with real information to scan.

Fraunces, weight 500

Mark

Reeds, or a sparkline — read either way.

Four uneven vertical strokes. As a wetland mark, they're reeds. As a data mark, they're the exact shape the cadence-tracking feature draws on screen.

Voice

State the fact. Skip the affirmation.

This is a tool for a business owner making decisions, not a client-facing wellness surface. No therapy-speak, no exclamation points.

Avoid

"Let's check in on your caring circle! 💛"

Use

"3 clients haven't rebooked in 30 days."

Avoid

"You're doing amazing, keep nurturing those connections!"

Use

"Dr. Mehta sent 3 referrals — last update was 61 days ago."

Avoid

"Oops! Looks like something went wrong on our end."

Use

"Couldn't save that change. Try again."
